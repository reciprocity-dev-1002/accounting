Running workarounds to get financial visibility. Business-owner identity. Willing to delegate. Growth or exit aware.

Channel Strategy
Priority 01
Referral Partners
Highest leverage. Warm intro at the moment of a major financial decision. Shortest path to conversion.
Priority 02
Cold Outbound
Cold email via HubSpot. Parallel track targeting established owners. Slower conversion cycle.
Priority 03
Content & Community
Blog + LinkedIn for partner outreach. Dental owner communities for long-cycle awareness. Builds inbound over time.
Referral Partner Categories
Category 01
Dental Transition Brokers
Already in the room when a dentist is buying a practice. Best referral moment: pre-close due diligence and post-close setup.
Category 02
Dental Attorneys
Handle the legal side of acquisitions and entity setup. Natural co-referral: dentist needs legal structure and clean books at the same time.
Category 03
Dental Masterminds & Consultants
Work with established owners on growth and operations. Refer when their clients need financial infrastructure, not just year-end compliance.
Anchor Entry Point for Referral Partners
Practice acquisition due diligence
Flat-fee ancillary offering. When a dentist is buying a practice, they need someone to review the books and tell them what they're actually buying — before they sign. Brokers and attorneys are already handling the transaction. This is a natural, low-friction referral that creates an ongoing client relationship.
Why it works
Acute pain. Real deadline. The dentist is already spending money on advisors. The referral partner looks like a value-add — not a sales pitch. Converts to ongoing monthly bookkeeping.
Sales Approach
Methodology
Integrity Selling. Explore implications. Test commitment. Give exit ramps. No pressure. No rebuttals.
Qualification
Qualify out non-fits early. A bad client costs more than a missed sale. Ohio-based, right practice size, willing to delegate.
Discovery
Calendly for scheduling. Focus on their current financial pain and what monthly clarity would change operationally.
