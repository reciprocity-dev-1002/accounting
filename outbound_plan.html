02  ·  Why Cold Outbound
Referral partners are the highest-leverage channel and always will be. But referrals take time to compound — brokers, attorneys, and consultants need to know us, trust us, and have a reason to mention us. Cold outbound runs in parallel: it reaches established owner-operators directly, without waiting for a partner to introduce us, and it produces market intelligence (what lands, what doesn’t) that sharpens every other channel. The goal is not volume — it’s booked discovery calls with owners who fit the target profile exactly.
03  ·  Who We’re Targeting
Target profile
Independent dental practice owner — 1–3 providers, $700K–$2M annual collections. Currently served by a generalist CPA with no monthly close, no dental benchmarks, and no practice visibility. Growth or exit aware. Delegates willingly.
Include
Owner-operators, 5+ years established, general or specialty (ortho, pedo, oral surg)
Exclude
DSO-owned, corporate-affiliated, new graduates, startups, practices with in-house bookkeeping
Tagging
HubSpot custom property “Outbound Cohort” = state + batch (e.g. OH-01, UT-01, OK-01)

Conservative B2B cold email benchmarks for owner-operator services in the $1,500–$2,500/mo price band. Sales cycle is 2–6 weeks from first touch to signed engagement — which is why April closes come from Mike’s warm referrals, not the cold list.

06  ·  List Build Approach
Target output: ~1,000 enriched contacts, 333 per state, owner-identified, verified email, imported to HubSpot and tagged by cohort. Five-step build:
01
Pull NPI universe (OH + UT + OK)
NPPES Registry bulk export, filter by taxonomy code 1223G0001X (General Dentistry) + relevant specialty codes. Expected output: ~9,800 records. Free.
02
Filter out DSO-affiliated practices
Clay AI research pass on each practice website to classify as DSO vs independent. Drops ~10–20% of universe.
03
Enrich with owner name + email (Clay waterfall)
Clay runs Apollo → Hunter → ZoomInfo → Clearbit in sequence, pays per match. Identifies owner-operator name + business email.
04
Verify with NeverBounce
Email deliverability scrub before HubSpot import. Protects sender reputation. ~$10 per 1,000 records.
05
Import to HubSpot & tag cohort
Custom property “Outbound Cohort” = OH-01 / UT-01 / OK-01. Designate as marketing contacts (counts against 1,000-contact Starter tier).
07  ·  Outreach Sequence
Three-touch email cadence. Each touch has distinct purpose and copy — no repeat messages. Sent Tuesday–Thursday, 7:30–9:00am or 11:00am–12:30pm. State-specific subject lines for local relevance.
Day 0  ·  Intro
Specific pain + state reference
Open with a specific observation about dental practice financials (insurance lag, cash vs accrual, overhead benchmarks). Reference state/metro for local signal. One-line CTA to a 20-minute call.
Day 4  ·  Value
Link to useful resource
Share a blog post or benchmark reference. Useful even if they never reply. Reinforces “we’re not generic” positioning. Soft CTA.
Day 9  ·  Break-up
Respectful exit
“Sounds like not the right time — I’ll stop reaching out.” Counterintuitively high reply rates. Leaves a positive impression even from a no.

09  ·  Success Metrics
Weekly
Emails sent · open rate · reply rate · unsubscribe rate. Target: 30%+ open, 3%+ reply, <0.5% unsub.
30-day check
Discovery calls booked (target 3–5). If <2, rewrite copy or re-segment. Don’t double down on a broken sequence.
60–90 day decision
1–3 clients closed = working. Scale to 1,500–2,000 new contacts/cycle. 0 closed = stop cold, pour resources into referrals.
10  ·  Open Questions for Reviewers
